## Core Terms

<TwoColumn>
  <Panel title="Study">
    The container you create. It includes the goal, format, context, questions, and follow-up settings.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Session">
    One completed participant conversation inside a study.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Responses">
    The table where completed sessions appear. Use it to inspect summaries, answers, status, and transcripts.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Transcript">
    The source wording from a participant conversation. Use it to verify what was actually said.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Insights">
    The synthesis view for the study. Use it to read patterns, sample information, numbers, and quotes.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Research">
    The place to ask questions across the collected study data.
  </Panel>
</TwoColumn>

## How The Pieces Connect

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Create a study

The study defines what Lemma should ask and how much it should follow up.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Participants complete sessions

Each completed conversation becomes one session in the study.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Responses show the source evidence

Open Responses to review sessions, summaries, transcripts, and per-question answers.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Insights and Research help you synthesize

Use Insights to read the generated synthesis. Use Research to ask specific questions about the study data.

</Step>
</Steps>

## FAQs & Common Issues

### Is a response the same as a session?

In practice, a completed participant conversation appears as a session in Responses.

### Where should I verify an important quote?

Open the transcript for the session.

### Where should I ask a question across all participants?

Use Research.
