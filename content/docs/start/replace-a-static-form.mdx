---
title: Replace a static form
description: Decide when an existing Typeform or Google Forms workflow should become a Lemma study.
---

Start with a form where the first answer is usually not enough.

Lemma is a strong replacement when you currently collect short responses and then wish you could ask follow-up questions, clarify examples, or turn the answers into Doc, Slides, transcripts, and a useful synthesis.

## Good replacement candidates

<TwoColumn>
  <Panel title="Customer feedback">
    Static forms tell you what people say. Lemma helps uncover why they said it, what happened, and what to fix first.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Lead qualification">
    Static forms collect fields. Lemma can ask about context, urgency, objections, fit, and next steps.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Churn feedback">
    Static forms collect cancellation reasons. Lemma can follow up on the trigger moment, failed workarounds, and what would have changed the decision.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Customer stories">
    Static forms collect fragments. Lemma can ask for the before state, turning point, outcome, quote, and proof.
  </Panel>
</TwoColumn>

## Weak replacement candidates

Keep a static form when the workflow is mostly operational:

- newsletter signup
- event registration
- file upload
- payment collection
- scheduling fields
- binary yes or no intake
- legally required structured fields

## How to choose the first form

Use this test:

1. Does the form support a real decision?
2. Would one follow-up question improve most answers?
3. Would a transcript, quote, theme, or summary be useful?
4. Would a human normally need to follow up manually?

If the answer is yes to at least three, it is a good Lemma candidate.

<ExampleBlock title="Example">
Instead of asking "Why did you cancel?" in a static field, use Lemma to ask what triggered the cancellation, what the customer tried first, what got worse over time, and what would have changed the outcome.
</ExampleBlock>

## What to preserve from the old form

When rebuilding a form as a Lemma study, preserve:

- the audience
- the decision the form supports
- the most important starter questions
- any language respondents already understand

Do not preserve every field. A study should not feel like a spoken spreadsheet.
