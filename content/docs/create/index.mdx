Design work in Lemma starts with the decision you want to support, then turns that decision into a study participants can complete without a live interviewer.

<Outcome title="Design principle">
A good Lemma study does not ask every possible question. It gives the AI interviewer enough context to pursue useful reasons, examples, objections, tradeoffs, and quotes.
</Outcome>

## Make The Key Design Choices

<Steps>
<Step>

### Choose text or voice

Voice is better when tone, story, hesitation, and detail matter. Text is better when participants need a quieter or lower-friction written path.

</Step>
<Step>

### Choose follow-up depth

Quick pulse is for a light signal. Standard interview is the default. Deep dive is for higher-stakes decisions that need more probing.

</Step>
<Step>

### Guide the follow-ups

Tell Lemma what counts as useful evidence. Ask for examples, constraints, failed attempts, tradeoffs, or the next action you need to decide.

</Step>
<Step>

### Test before sharing

Preview the experience and check whether the prompts would produce usable evidence, not just complete responses.

</Step>
</Steps>
