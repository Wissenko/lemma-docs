<HelpCallout type="insight" title="A good study is not a long survey">
A good Lemma study gives the AI interviewer enough context to pursue useful reasons, examples, objections, tradeoffs, and quotes.
</HelpCallout>

## Before You Start

Finish this sentence:

> We need these responses so we can decide...

Good study goals name:

- the audience
- the situation they experienced
- the decision your team needs to make
- the evidence that would change the decision

For example: understand why recently churned customers cancelled, what they tried before leaving, and what would have changed their decision.

## How To Design The Study

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Choose text or voice

Use **voice conversation** when stories, nuance, emotion, hesitation, or long qualitative answers matter. Use **text conversation** when participants need a quieter, lower-friction written path.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Choose follow-up depth

Use **Quick pulse** for a fast signal, **Standard interview** for most research and feedback studies, and **Deep dive** when the decision needs richer probing.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Add context or a guide

Use the brain dump field when you know the goal but have not written a guide. Upload a discussion guide when you already have a structured research plan.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Use a template when the workflow is familiar

Templates are useful for common workflows like customer discovery, satisfaction, product or service feedback, and employee experience.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 5: Shape the starter questions

Starter questions should invite a story, reason, example, expectation, or tradeoff. They should not flatten the study into a static survey.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 6: Guide the follow-ups

Tell Lemma what counts as useful depth. Ask it to follow up on concrete examples, failed workarounds, objections, switching moments, constraints, or next steps.

</Step>
</Steps>

## What Good Looks Like

By the time you launch, the study should make clear:

- who should answer
- what decision the study supports
- whether participants answer by text or voice
- how much probing Lemma should do
- what details are worth following up on
- what output the team needs after responses come in

<HelpCallout type="tip" title="Check the first question">
If the first question could belong in any generic survey, tighten the study goal before launching.
</HelpCallout>

## FAQs & Common Issues

### What if I only have a rough idea?

Use the brain dump field. Include the audience, the decision, what you already know, what you are unsure about, and what a useful answer would include.

### Should I ask demographic questions first?

Only ask them if they affect the decision. If the audience is already known through Contacts or Segments, keep the participant flow focused on the evidence you need.

### How do I avoid shallow answers?

Ask for examples, recent moments, attempted workarounds, constraints, tradeoffs, and what changed. Avoid yes-or-no questions unless the follow-up is the real point.
