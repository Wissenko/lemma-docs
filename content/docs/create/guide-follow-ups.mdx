---
title: Guide follow-up questions
description: Tell Lemma what to listen for so it can ask better follow-up questions.
---

Follow-up guidance tells Lemma what kind of depth matters.

This is the main difference between Lemma and a static form. A static form captures the first answer. Lemma should get the reason, example, objection, tradeoff, emotion, and next step behind it.

## Start with what you want to uncover

Write a short list of details that would make the response more useful.

For customer feedback:

- the task the customer was trying to complete
- where the experience broke down
- what they expected instead
- how often the issue happens
- what they would fix first

For lead qualification:

- current workflow
- urgency
- problem severity
- buying context
- objections
- next step

For churn:

- trigger moment
- failed workaround
- product, pricing, support, timing, or fit
- what would have changed the decision
- whether the customer might return

## Use specific follow-up targets

Weak:

> Ask good follow-up questions.

Better:

> Follow up when the participant mentions pricing, onboarding delays, manual workarounds, missing integrations, stakeholder objections, or switching to another tool.

<ExampleBlock title="Example guidance for churn">
If the participant says pricing was the reason, ask what changed in perceived value before price became a problem. If they mention a missing feature, ask when that missing feature first blocked their workflow and what workaround they tried.
</ExampleBlock>

## Match depth to the study

If the study is a Quick pulse, keep the guidance narrow. If the study is a Deep dive, include more follow-up targets and examples.
