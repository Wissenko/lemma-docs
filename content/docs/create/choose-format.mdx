## Use voice conversation when

Voice is usually better when you need:

- richer qualitative answers
- emotional nuance
- stories and examples
- less typing friction for long answers
- a research or interview-like experience

Voice is a strong fit for customer discovery, churn follow-up, customer stories, employee experience, and feedback that needs the reason behind the first answer.

## Use text conversation when

Text is usually better when:

- participants may not be able to speak
- answers should be shorter
- the topic is operational or structured
- participants need to answer in a quiet or shared workspace
- you want lower friction for quick written feedback

## What changes in the docs

The marketing shorthand is "voice form," but the app supports both text and voice conversations. In the docs, use:

- **study** for the thing you create
- **voice conversation** when the participant speaks
- **text conversation** when the participant writes
