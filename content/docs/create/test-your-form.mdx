---
title: Test or preview a study
description: Check the participant experience and output expectations before sharing a study.
---

Test every study before you share it widely.

The goal is not to produce a perfect answer. The goal is to catch confusing wording, missing context, weak follow-up guidance, and outputs that are too vague to use.

## What to check

<Steps>
<Step>

### The opening makes sense

The participant should understand why they are being asked to answer and what kind of response is useful.

</Step>
<Step>

### The format matches the task

Voice should feel natural for longer qualitative answers. Text should feel appropriate for shorter written input.

</Step>
<Step>

### Follow-ups add depth

Good follow-ups ask for reasons, examples, expectations, tradeoffs, or next steps.

</Step>
<Step>

### The output will support a decision

After sessions complete, the response table, transcript, Doc, Slides, or Research Agent should make the answers easier to act on.

</Step>
</Steps>

## Fix common issues

<TwoColumn>
  <Panel title="If the conversation feels vague">
    Tighten the goal and add clearer follow-up targets.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="If the participant may be confused">
    Rewrite the opening question in plain language.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="If follow-ups feel too long">
    Lower the follow-up depth or narrow the study goal.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="If the expected output feels generic">
    Add the decision the study should support and the evidence you need.
  </Panel>
</TwoColumn>
