Follow-up depth controls how much Lemma should ask after a participant gives an initial answer.

## How To Choose Follow-Up Depth

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Open the follow-up depth menu

Use the **Follow-up depth** control when creating the study.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Choose the study depth

Choose **Quick pulse**, **Standard interview**, or **Deep dive**.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Review the generated questions

After Lemma drafts the study, check whether the number and depth of questions fit the goal.

</Step>
</Steps>

## What Each Depth Means

<TwoColumn>
  <Panel title="Quick pulse">
    Use this for a fast signal. It is designed for fewer questions and minimal follow-ups.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Standard interview">
    Use this for most studies. It balances useful probing with a reasonable participant experience.
  </Panel>
  <Panel title="Deep dive">
    Use this when the study needs richer probing and more detail.
  </Panel>
</TwoColumn>

## FAQs & Common Issues

### Should I always choose Deep dive?

No. Use Deep dive only when the study needs more detail. More depth can make the experience longer.

### Can I control follow-ups per question?

Yes. In the question editor, you can use the AI follow-up toggle, choose follow-up depth, and add follow-up instructions.

### What depth should I use for a first study?

Use **Standard interview** unless you already know the study should be very short or very deep.
