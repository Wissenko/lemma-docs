## How To Edit Questions

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Open the questions step

After the study context is ready, generate or open the interview questions.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Review each starter question

Make sure each question is specific enough to guide the participant.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Turn AI follow-ups on or off

Use **AI follow-up questions** for questions where Lemma should probe beyond the first answer.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Choose question-level depth

Choose the follow-up depth for that question, such as **Light**, **Medium**, or **Deep**.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 5: Add follow-up instructions

Use the follow-up instructions field to tell Lemma what kind of detail to pursue.

</Step>
</Steps>

## Good Follow-Up Instructions

Use instructions that name the kind of evidence you want. For example:

- ask for a concrete example
- ask what happened before the decision
- ask what made the answer frustrating or useful
- ask what the participant tried before
- ask what would have changed the outcome

## FAQs & Common Issues

### Should every question have AI follow-ups?

No. Use follow-ups where the first answer is likely to need more context.

### What makes a question too broad?

If the question could fit any study, make it more specific to the audience, situation, or decision.

### Can I give different follow-up instructions per question?

Yes. Use question-level instructions when each question needs a different kind of probing.
