Launch work is about getting the right people into the study with the right expectation.

A Lemma study should feel like a lightweight link for participants, but it can collect evidence closer to a short interview. Set that expectation when you share it.

## Before You Launch

Confirm that:

- the title matches the participant-facing topic
- the format is right for the audience
- the follow-up depth matches the decision risk
- the first question is concrete enough
- the respondent-facing link is the link you plan to share

## How To Launch The Study

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Confirm the study setup

Check that the title, prompts, format, and follow-up depth match the decision you need to make.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Choose the audience

Send the study directly, or use Contacts and Segments when the audience should be managed from participant data.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Share the participant link

Use the study link when participants should complete the study on their own time. Do not send internal app URLs when participants need the respondent-facing flow.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Set expectations

Tell participants why you are asking, whether the study is text or voice, roughly how long it will take, and that Lemma may ask follow-up questions.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 5: Monitor collection

Use the study overview and Responses table to see who joined, who completed, and whether the sessions are producing enough evidence.

</Step>
</Steps>

## Invite Copy Pattern

Use plain language that helps participants give concrete answers:

> We are using Lemma to collect short interview-style feedback. It should take about five minutes. You will answer a few questions, and Lemma may ask follow-ups so we can understand the details behind your answer.

<HelpCallout type="tip" title="Name the reason for the study">
Participants give better answers when they know why their feedback matters and what kind of decision it will inform.
</HelpCallout>

## FAQs & Common Issues

### What link should I send?

Send the respondent-facing study link, not the internal app URL.

### Should I mention that Lemma may ask follow-ups?

Yes. This helps participants understand why the experience feels more like a short conversation than a static form.

### When should I stop collecting?

Stop when you have enough evidence to support the decision or when new responses are repeating the same patterns without adding new detail.
