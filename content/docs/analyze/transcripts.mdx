## How To Open A Transcript

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Open Responses

Go to the study and open **Responses**.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Find the session

Use the row for the participant or session you want to inspect.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Click View in the transcript column

Open the transcript to read the conversation turns.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Compare the transcript with the summary

Use the transcript to verify whether a summary, quote, or finding is grounded in the source.

</Step>
</Steps>

## When To Use Transcripts

- a quote is important
- a summary feels too broad
- a participant answer needs context
- a finding needs source evidence
- you want to understand the conversation flow

## FAQs & Common Issues

### Should I read every transcript first?

Not always. Start with Responses or Insights, then open transcripts for important or unclear evidence.

### Are transcripts the source of truth?

They are the best place to verify the wording and context of a session.

### Where should I go after checking a transcript?

Return to Responses, Insights, or Research depending on the question you are trying to answer.
