Insights is the report-style view for a study. Use it to review sample details, key numbers, findings, exhibits, and quotes.

## How To Read Insights

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Open the study

Open the study from the Studies dashboard.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Open Insights

Use the **Insights** tab.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Check the study metadata

Review the sample, method, field period, and issued date.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Review the numbers

Use the "By the numbers" area to understand the size and shape of the study.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 5: Read findings with the evidence in mind

Use highlighted quotes and transcripts to verify important claims.

</Step>
</Steps>

## What Insights Can Show

- sample information
- method and field period
- completed interviews or sessions
- recorded conversation time
- participant roles or functions
- findings
- exhibits or charts
- highlighted quotes

## FAQs & Common Issues

### Should I treat Insights as final truth?

Treat it as synthesis. Verify important findings against Responses and transcripts.

### What should I check first?

Check the sample and method before reading the findings.

### Where should I ask follow-up questions about the synthesis?

Use Research.
