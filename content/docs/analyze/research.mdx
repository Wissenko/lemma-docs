## How To Use Research

<Steps>
<Step>

### Step 1: Open the study

Open the study that has collected sessions.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 2: Open Research

Use the **Research** tab.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 3: Ask a specific question

Ask about patterns, contradictions, priorities, or the most important insight from the study.

</Step>
<Step>

### Step 4: Check important claims

Use Responses and transcripts to verify important evidence.

</Step>
</Steps>

## Good Research Questions

- What is the most important insight from this study?
- Are there contradictions between participants?
- What patterns appear across completed sessions?
- Which participant quotes best support this finding?
- What should I inspect in the transcripts?

## FAQs & Common Issues

### When should I use Research instead of Insights?

Use Insights when you want the generated synthesis. Use Research when you have a specific question.

### Should I ask broad questions?

Specific questions usually produce more useful answers.

### Where should I verify Research answers?

Use Responses and transcripts.
